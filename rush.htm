Brotherhood
As a brother of Delta Tau Delta, you will be surrounded by people who will truly be your friends. Brotherhood in Delta Tau Delta is unlike anything you're ever experienced - you will be among a diverse group of men, where you will always be able to find someone to share you interests with, regardless of where they may lie. When you become a Delt, you become part of both a local and national organization that will guide you to live the life of a Delt; you will learn to live the Delt Mission: Committed to Lives of Excellence. You will find yourself in a vast network across the nation, able to find a fellow brother wherever you may be.

Academics
One of the greatest concerns many rushees have when looking at prospective fraternities is: "I don't want to do poorly in school if I join a fraternity." At Delta Tau Delta, academics are one of our greatest concerns, and you will see this as soon as your pledgeship. We offer sessions twice a week where actives and pledges alike can go for academic help in any of their subjects. We have members that are majoring in almost every subject and will undoubtly be able to help you with whatever it is you need.

Philanthropy
There is nothing better than the feeling one gets when they give to those in need. We understand that those who are not as fortunate as us, and we take it as our personal responsibility to provide whatever help we can. That is why the members of Delta Tau Delta are very much involved in many different types of philanthropic events. On almost any given weekend, you can be sure to find at least one member participating in some sort of philanthropic event - weather it be individually or through another greek organization. However, our community involvement doesn't stop there. As a whole house, we organize yearly events to give to those in need. During our largest event, Delt Games, the entire house runs a competition where sororities compete in events throughout the week. The proceeds are donated to the Tucson Food Bank and a local shelter for battered women. In our 2005 Delt Games, we collected 2,275 pounds of canned food, and raised over $3000 dollars.
